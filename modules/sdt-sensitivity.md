# Sensitivity

:::{note} Where you are
A subsection of *Signal Detection Theory*, following on from
[the criterion](sdt-criterion.md) (1d).
:::

We saw that an observer's accuracy depends on where they place their criterion. But
it also depends on how well they can actually tell signal from noise — their
**sensitivity**. This subsection builds that idea up in three short steps:

::::{grid} 1 1 3 3
:::{card} 1 · Relation to accuracy
:link: sdt-sensitivity-accuracy.md
Two observers can reach the *same* proportion correct for completely different
reasons.
:::
:::{card} 2 · What is the inverse normal?
:link: sdt-sensitivity-norminv.md
A quick look at `norminv`, the function we need to measure sensitivity.
:::
:::{card} 3 · d-prime
:link: sdt-sensitivity-dprime.md
A measure of sensitivity that separates genuine detection from response bias.
:::
::::

You can read them in order, or dip into whichever you like.
