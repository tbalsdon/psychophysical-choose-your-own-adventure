# Psychometrics and the internal value

In [Signal Detection Theory](sdt-intro.md) we examined two key concepts:

- External signals are added on a background of 'noise', meaning that we do not
  experience discrete states; rather, our psychological experience varies along some
  internal continuum.
- We infer the cause of our psychological experience (whether an external signal was
  presented) by placing a criterion on this internal continuum, and the placement of
  this criterion is under our strategic control.

Here we delve deeper into what this internal continuum is, in contexts beyond signal
detection, and how we measure it.
