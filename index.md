# Welcome 👋

These tutorials measure the relationship between **physical stimuli** and our
**psychological experience** of them — and what that relationship tells us about
the processes underlying perception and decision-making.

> "It is an unavoidable aspect of psychological experiments that no matter how
> well instructed, it is the observer who determines how the task is done."
> — Galvin et al., 2003

Every page mixes short explanations with **live code you can edit and re-run in
your browser** — no installation, no account. Change a parameter, press run, and
watch the behaviour change.

## How to use these tutorials

There is a suggested order, but you don't have to follow it. Each page links to
related ideas, so you can wander wherever your curiosity takes you — a bit like a
Wikipedia rabbit-hole. The [module map](sitemap.md) shows how everything connects.

:::{tip} This is a proof-of-concept
:class: dropdown
This site currently contains **one** finished module — [](modules/sdt-noisy-continuum.md) —
as a demonstration of the format. The full set of ~25 modules (signal detection
theory, psychometrics, scales, methods of manipulation, plus diffusion models and
Bayesian inference) will follow.
:::
